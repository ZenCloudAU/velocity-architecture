# SLT Discovery Workshop
## Architecture Decision-Flow Assessment

**ZenCloud Global Consultants**  
Classification: Engagement Use — Not for Distribution

---

## Purpose

This workshop establishes a shared understanding of how the client organisation currently makes, records, and governs architecture decisions.

It is not an audit. It is a diagnostic conversation.

The output is a clear picture of where decision-flow breaks down — and which Velocity engagement model best addresses the gap.

**This workshop is the entry point to every ZenCloud engagement.**

---

## Attendees

**Required:**
- Enterprise Architect or Architecture Lead
- Solution Architect representative (at least one active program)
- PMO Lead or Program Director

**Strongly recommended:**
- CTO, CIO, or their delegate
- Head of Delivery or equivalent

**Facilitator:** Phil Myint, Principal Architect, ZenCloud Global Consultants

**Note to partner:** Introductions and context-setting can be owned by the partner. ZenCloud leads from the diagnostic questions onward.

**Ideal group size:** 4–8 people. Larger groups produce less candid responses. Split into two sessions if required.

---

## Pre-Read for Attendees

Send to participants 48 hours before the workshop:

> *This is a 90-minute working session — not a presentation. We will ask structured questions about how your organisation makes and records architecture decisions. There are no right answers. The goal is to understand where your current model creates friction, and where a decision-first approach would add the most value.*
>
> *Come prepared to talk about:*  
> *– A recent major architecture decision and how it was made*  
> *– Whether that decision is documented and traceable today*  
> *– How your PMO currently interacts with your architecture function*

No slides required from participants. No preparation beyond thinking about the above.

**ZenCloud:**

---

## Block 2: The Velocity Thesis — 10-Minute Framing (15 minutes)

ZenCloud delivers a short, focused framing. No full deck.

**Key points to land:**

1. Most organisations do not have an artefact problem. They have a decision-flow problem.
2. The conventional sequence is Design → Document → Govern → Deliver. The Velocity sequence is Decide → Record → Govern → Artefact.
3. Velocity connects Enterprise Architecture, Business Architecture, Solution Architecture, and PMO / Program Delivery into one decision-first operating model.
4. AI-assisted tools accelerate the practitioner. They do not replace practitioner accountability.

**What this is not:**
- A pitch for a new tool
- A claim that the organisation no longer needs architects
- An offer to take over the architecture function

**What this is:**
- A structured method for making architecture decisions explicit, traceable, and board-ready
- An offer to help the organisation get more value from the architecture work it is already doing

Allow 3–4 minutes for immediate reactions before moving to the diagnostic.

## Block 3: Decision-Flow Assessment (25 minutes)

The core diagnostic block. Facilitator asks questions. Participants respond. ZenCloud listens and notes.

Do not rush. Three or four genuine answers are more valuable than covering every question.

**Opening question — warm up:**

> *"Think about the last significant architecture decision your organisation made — something that had a meaningful impact on a program or platform. Can you describe how that decision was made?"*

Let the answer run. Note who made the decision, how the options were evaluated, and whether the outcome was recorded.

---

### Decision Clarity Prompts

> *"If I asked you to name the five most important architecture decisions governing your current major programs — could you list them? Are they documented anywhere accessible?"*

> *"When a program diverges from its intended design, what is the mechanism for understanding why? Is that traceable to a decision?"*

> *"How are architecture options evaluated before a decision is made? Is there a structured criteria set — or is it mostly judgment and consensus?"*

> *"Who has the authority to make a significant architecture decision? Is that authority explicit — or assumed?"*

---

### Governance Speed Prompts

> *"How long does it typically take to get an architecture decision through governance — from the question being raised to an approved outcome?"*

> *"When a governance gate is reached, what does the board or committee actually evaluate? Is it the decision logic — or the document format?"*

> *"Has a governance process ever slowed down a delivery to the point where the decision became irrelevant by the time it was approved?"*

---

### Traceability Prompts

> *"If a new architect joins a program tomorrow, how long would it take them to understand the decision trail — why the architecture looks the way it does today?"*

> *"Is there a decision register for your current major programs? If yes — when was it last updated? If no — where would you look to reconstruct the decision history?"*

> *"If the board asked you to explain the top three risk trade-offs accepted in your current architecture — could you answer that today, and show the evidence?"*

---

## Block 4: Artefact and Repository Assessment (15 minutes)

**Purpose:** Understand whether the artefact problem is about volume, quality, access, or currency.

---

### Artefact Usefulness Prompts

> *"What architecture documents are currently maintained for your major programs? Who reads them after sign-off?"*

> *"Are your artefacts produced before decisions are made — or to document decisions that have already been made informally?"*

> *"If you needed to find the rationale behind a specific architecture choice made 12 months ago — where would you look? How long would that take?"*

---

### Repository and Access Prompts

> *"Where do your architecture artefacts live? SharePoint? Confluence? Email attachments? Somewhere else?"*

> *"Who has access to the architecture repository? Is it architecture-team only — or visible to delivery and PMO?"*

> *"When was the last time the repository was audited for currency? Are there documents in there that no longer reflect how the architecture actually works?"*

---

### Architecture / Delivery Alignment Prompts

> *"Does your delivery team know what architecture decisions govern their sprint work? How is that communicated?"*

> *"When an architecture decision changes mid-program, how does that change get communicated to delivery? Is there a formal mechanism?"*

> *"Has architecture drift — where delivery has moved away from the design without a recorded decision — been a problem? Can you give an example?"*

---

## Block 5: PMO, Governance, Data, Security, and AI Prompts (15 minutes)

**Purpose:** Assess the four remaining maturity dimensions and probe for specific high-value opportunities.

Select 2–3 prompts per area based on what has already emerged in the conversation. Do not attempt all of them.

---

### PMO and Governance Prompts

> *"Does your PMO reference architecture decisions in program governance reporting? Is architecture status part of the standard status report — or handled separately?"*

> *"When a program reaches a key delivery milestone, is there an architecture checkpoint? What does that checkpoint evaluate?"*

> *"Who in the PMO understands the architecture well enough to flag when delivery is diverging from design?"*

---

### Data Readiness Prompts

> *"For your current major programs — is the data required to execute the design available, clean, and governed? Or is data quality a discovery item mid-program?"*

> *"Has a program ever stalled because the data wasn't in the state that the architecture assumed it would be? What happened?"*

> *"If you are evaluating AI adoption — have you assessed whether your data is in a state that supports the AI use case you are considering?"*

---

### Security Integration Prompts

> *"At what point in an architecture engagement does security get involved? Is it a decision input — or a sign-off gate at the end?"*

> *"Are security risk trade-offs documented as decisions — with an owner and a review date — or are they signed off and filed?"*

> *"Has a security concern ever emerged late in a program that could have been addressed earlier if security was part of the architecture decision process?"*

---

### AI Readiness Prompts

> *"Is your organisation evaluating or implementing AI? If yes — who is making the architecture decisions about how AI is adopted, and how are those decisions being governed?"*

> *"Do you have a governance model for AI architecture decisions — or are AI adoption decisions being made at the product or team level without architecture oversight?"*

> *"If a business unit asked to use a public AI tool with enterprise data tomorrow — what would happen? Is there a decision framework that governs that?"*

---

## Block 6: Outputs and Next Steps (10 minutes)

ZenCloud summarises the session — verbally, in the room.

**What to cover:**

1. **What we heard:** Two or three key patterns from the diagnostic. Be specific. Use language the participants used.

2. **Where the decision-flow breaks down:** Name the dimension or dimensions where the maturity gap is largest. Do not score — this is a conversation, not a report card.

3. **What Velocity addresses:** Match the gap to the relevant engagement model. Do not pitch all five models. Recommend one.

4. **Proposed next step:** One of:
   - A 4-week Decision Sprint on a defined architecture question
   - A Decision Maturity Assessment (full VDMA workshop, separate session)
   - An embedded advisory conversation for longer programs

5. **Administrative close:**
   - Who is the point of contact for next steps?
   - What is the decision timeline?
   - Any data or confidentiality questions to address before proceeding?

## Facilitation Notes

- Do not fill silence. Questions that produce silence are often the most revealing.
- If participants describe a symptom (documents not read, slow governance), press for the root cause (how are decisions being made?) before moving on.
- If the conversation reveals that architecture is functioning well in one area but poorly in another, name it. The diagnostic value is in the specificity.
- If a participant becomes defensive, name the observation without attribution: *"It sounds like there may be some tension between the pace governance requires and the pace delivery needs — is that fair?"*
- The goal is not to close a sale in the room. The goal is to produce a credible next step the partner can convert.
