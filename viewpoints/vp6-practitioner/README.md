# VP6 — Practitioner Viewpoint

**Instrument:** Integrity Arc (Auctor Custos)  
**Layer:** Human — practitioner integrity  
**ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 conformant viewpoint**

---

## Purpose

The Practitioner Viewpoint governs the conduct of the architectural practitioner.

It distinguishes **authorship** from document production. It defines the conditions under which an architect sets boundaries, refuses to convert indecision into output, and — when necessary — exits with integrity intact.

The practitioner role in the VAF is named **Auctor Custos** — author-custodian. The architect who guards the integrity of the work rather than absorbing the organisation's unresolved conflict.

> *You cannot practice architecture with integrity in every system. Some environments are structurally opposed to clarity. In those systems, the architect has three options: become a document producer, become a buffer, or become an author and accept the consequences.*

## The Integrity Arc

The Integrity Arc describes the progression of practitioner positions in a system under pressure. It is not a linear path — it is a set of positions available to a practitioner at any point.

### 1. Authorship
Asserting intent into a constrained system. Not facilitating. Not documenting. Saying: *here is the shape of the decision, and here is why.*

Authorship begins where neutrality ends.

### 2. Boundaries
A boundary is a declaration of scope. It states, plainly and professionally, what architecture can and cannot be accountable for.

*Architecture can clarify decisions once they exist. It cannot manufacture courage, resolve political conflict by abstraction, or carry ambiguity indefinitely.*

### 3. Refusal
The precise act of stopping the conversion of indecision into output.

Refusal is not rebellion. It does not argue. It does not persuade. It simply stops producing artefacts from decisions that have not been made.

*Refusal sounds like clarity, not confrontation.*

### 4. Staying
Practising without illusion. Remaining engaged while no longer expecting that effort will convert to alignment.

Staying is provisional — continuously assessed, not permanently committed.

### 5. Exit
The last architectural decision still available. When continued participation would require misrepresentation — of progress, of intent, or of responsibility — exit preserves the discipline's integrity.

*Architecture that cannot leave when it must is not architecture. It is service work for indecision.*

## Sacred Cows Register

Sacred cows are organisational beliefs that resist clarity by protecting dysfunction over design. They must be named before they can be addressed.

| Sacred Cow | How it manifests |
|------------|-----------------|
| Effort equals progress | Exhaustion reframed as commitment; sacrifice normalised as leadership |
| Ambiguity protects leadership | Vague direction allows blame to float; clarity is avoided because it creates accountability |
| Governance as performance | Reviews held for auditors, not decisions; ceremony substitutes for control |
| Pragmatism as abdication | "Just be pragmatic" used to authorise proceeding without decisions |
| Helpfulness as complicity | Refusing to refuse is reframed as professionalism |

## Extended Domain Note

In the leadership domain, the practitioner is any authority responsible for convergence. The Integrity Arc applies without modification. The leader as Auctor Custos is the direct equivalent of the architect as Auctor Custos.
