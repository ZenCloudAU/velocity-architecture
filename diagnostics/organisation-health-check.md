# Organisation Health Check

**VAF — Structural Pathology Diagnostic**

> Use this diagnostic to identify which VAF pathologies are present in your organisation.  
> It is distinct from the Decision Velocity Diagnostic (which measures capability).  
> This one measures structural dysfunction.

---

## How to Use This

This diagnostic is most useful when completed by multiple people independently and compared. Divergence in scores is itself diagnostic — it reveals where perception and reality have separated.

Score each statement:
- **0** — Not true
- **1** — Occasionally true
- **2** — Often true
- **3** — Consistently and reliably true

## Interpretation

| Total | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 0–20 | **Healthy** — Isolated issues. Use VAF to formalise and strengthen what is working. |
| 21–40 | **Emerging dysfunction** — Patterns are forming. Intervene before they embed. |
| 41–60 | **Embedded dysfunction** — Multiple pathologies reinforcing each other. Structural intervention required. |
| 61–90 | **Systemic** — The organisation has been redesigned for inaction. Begin with the Foundation Layer and VP6. |

## Section-by-Section Response

| Highest-scoring section | Primary intervention |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Section 1 — Responsibility diffusion | VP4: Name every open decision with a single owner. Deploy the 14-Day Aging Rule. |
| Section 2 — Alignment as defence | VP2 + VP5: Replace the next alignment meeting with a Trade-off Matrix. |
| Section 3 — Infrastructure of diffusion | VP5: Redesign governance as Pulse sessions. Enforce CR-E3 terminal conditions. |
| Section 4 — Incentive geometry | Leadership: Name the incentive geometry. Make decisiveness visible and rewarded. |
| Section 5 — Architecture Theatre | VP6: Practitioner integrity assessment. Name the sacred cows. |
| Section 6 — Sacred cows | VP6 + Foundation Layer: Name each sacred cow explicitly. Apply the Integrity Arc. |

## The One Question

If you do nothing else with this diagnostic, answer this question in writing and share it:

> *What would your organisation have to give up — in comfort, in cover, or in control — to fix this properly?*

That answer is the sacred cow worth naming first.
