# Velocity as the AI Architecture Coordination Layer

## Thesis

Architecture domains are not enough. Artefact catalogues are not enough. Agentic enterprises need coordination.

Velocity is the architecture coordination layer for decision-first, AI-assisted enterprise execution.

## Why Domains Are Not Enough

Traditional maps explain which architecture domains exist:

- enterprise architecture
- business architecture
- solution architecture
- data architecture
- security architecture
- PMO / delivery governance

The problem is that domains often operate in parallel, producing artefacts without resolving the decision flow between them.

## Why Artefact Catalogues Are Not Enough

Artefact catalogues can improve consistency, but they do not guarantee:

- decision ownership
- decision speed
- traceability
- execution alignment
- stakeholder clarity
- AI accountability
- PMO integration

Velocity treats artefacts as decision-support instruments, not as the goal.

## Velocity Coordination Role

Velocity coordinates:

- Enterprise Architecture
- Business Architecture
- Solution Architecture
- PMO / Program Delivery

Through cross-cutting lenses:

- business value
- data readiness
- security risk
- AI readiness
- governance and traceability

## Four-Box Operating Model

Velocity asks:

1. What decision needs to be made?
2. Which architecture domains must contribute?
3. Which artefacts are useful for the decision?
4. How is the decision traced into delivery?

## Hermes as Internal Routing Agent

Hermes supports the coordination layer by:

- classifying requests
- matching offers
- recommending artefacts
- selecting branding mode
- flagging data/AI risks
- routing to PMO Portal, VAF-SA, Academy, StudioSix, or partner workflow

Hermes does not make final decisions.

## White-Label Partner Delivery Support

Velocity can operate invisibly behind a partner:

- partner owns account and commercial relationship
- Phil / ZenCloud provides specialist architecture execution
- StudioSix supports production
- Velocity structures the decisions
- deliverables can use partner templates and naming

## FastStart Support

In Executive Architecture FastStart, Velocity provides:

- rapid decision-flow diagnosis
- architecture coordination maturity view
- executive decision map
- risk and traceability view
- 90-day action plan

## Decision Sprint Support

In Architecture Decision Sprint, Velocity provides:

- decision backlog
- stakeholder map
- artefact usefulness review
- governance cadence
- PMO/EA alignment model
- traceability model

## Metrics

Velocity Decision Metrics measure:

- decision cycle time
- decision quality
- traceability coverage
- artefact usefulness
- governance throughput
- execution alignment
- AI review compliance

## Risks and Controls

| Risk | Control |
|---|---|
| AI output treated as final | Human review gate. |
| Artefacts become the goal | Link every artefact to a decision. |
| Partner brand confusion | Use white-label rules. |
| Client data leakage | Use approved private storage only. |
| Governance slows delivery | Measure decision cycle time. |
| Architecture disconnected from PMO | Use PMO alignment artefacts. |
