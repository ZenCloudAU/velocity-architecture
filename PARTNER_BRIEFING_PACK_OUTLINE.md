## Purpose of This Document

This is the slide-by-slide outline for a partner briefing deck. It is designed to be adapted into a StudioSix-produced presentation for use in initial consultancy partner conversations.

The deck positions ZenCloud as the architecture advisory branch the partner does not have — operating through a structured, practitioner-led, AI-assisted model.

**Deck length:** 10–12 slides  
**Delivery format:** In-person or video call — 30-minute run, 30-minute discussion  
**Audience:** Practice leads, advisory directors, architecture capability heads

## Slide 2 — The Problem We Are Both Seeing

**Slide title:** Most organisations have a decision-flow problem, not an artefact problem

**Purpose:** Establish shared diagnostic ground. Partner has seen this in their clients. Name it.

**Key points:**
- Architecture reviews produce documents. Programs produce status reports. Decisions are buried or implicit.
- Governance gates approve format — not decision quality.
- Programs diverge from design. No traceable explanation.
- SLT cannot interrogate the decision trail of a major investment.
- The artefact trap: governance measures the document, not the decision.

**Suggested visual:** A simple split — left: "What governance measures" (list of document types); right: "What SLT needs" (list: clarity, risk, confidence, alignment). Visual contrast.

---

## Slide 3 — The Velocity Thesis

**Slide title:** Decision-first. Artefact-second.

**Purpose:** Introduce the core inversion. This is the intellectual anchor of the conversation.

**Key points:**
- Conventional: Design → Document → Govern → Deliver
- Velocity: Decide → Record → Govern → Artefact
- Every engagement begins with a decision question — not a diagram
- Options are evaluated. Decision is recorded. Artefact is derived from the decision.
- Result: architecture that is traceable, auditable, and aligned to business outcomes

**Suggested visual:** Two horizontal flow arrows — conventional (grey) vs Velocity (Signal Blue). Simple, clean.

## Slide 4 — The Four-Box Operating Model

**Slide title:** One operating model. Four domains. Five lenses.

**Purpose:** Show how Velocity connects domains the partner's clients typically silo.

**Key points:**
- Enterprise Architecture: strategic alignment, platform governance, standards
- Business Architecture: capabilities, value streams, operating model, benefits
- Solution Architecture: design decisions, trade-off analysis, integration patterns
- PMO / Program Delivery: governance cadence, risk and assumptions, decision registers
- Five cross-cutting lenses applied at every decision gate: Business Value, Data Readiness, Security Risk, AI Readiness, Governance & Traceability

**Suggested visual:** The four-box grid with the five lenses listed below or around it. Clean diagram — matches the Velocity brand mark.

## Slide 5 — What a Velocity Practitioner Produces

**Slide title:** Concrete outputs. Not reports about what could be done.

**Purpose:** Make the value tangible. Partner needs to know what the client receives.

**Key points:**
- Architecture Board Pack — executive-ready, decision-summarised
- Trade-off Matrix — options compared against defined criteria
- Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) — formal, traceable, auditable
- Risk Register and Assumptions Register — owned, dated, active
- Decision Register — full chronological log across the engagement
- PMO Governance Snapshot — architecture status aligned to delivery
- All outputs practitioner-reviewed before delivery

**Suggested visual:** Six output cards in a 2×3 grid. Each card: output name + one-line description. Icon per card.

## Slide 6 — Where AI Fits

**Slide title:** AI accelerates the practitioner. It does not replace accountability.

**Purpose:** Address the AI question before the partner asks it. Be precise.

**Key points:**
- VAF Agentic AI compresses drafting time for board packs, ADRs, registers, and trade-off analysis
- AI generates drafts. The practitioner reviews, validates, and owns every output.
- No confidential client data enters any public AI tool
- AI outputs are never delivered to a client without practitioner sign-off
- Client data does not train any AI model
- The Velocity operating model is human-led, AI-assisted

**Suggested visual:** Simple two-column layout: "AI does" (list: draft, generate, structure, accelerate) vs "Practitioner does" (list: review, validate, challenge, own, deliver). Clear visual distinction.

## Slide 7 — The Partnership Model

**Slide title:** How ZenCloud works through a partner

**Purpose:** Define the operating structure so the partner can see where they fit.

**Key points:**
- Partner owns the client relationship and commercial terms
- ZenCloud provides architecture advisory capability — decision-first, AI-assisted, practitioner-led
- White-label or named delivery — agreed per engagement
- ZenCloud does not compete for the client relationship; it deepens the partner's architecture offer
- StudioSix provides production support (presentations, white papers, training content) behind the advisory layer
- Escalation model: delivery risks to ZenCloud → ZenCloud to partner lead → partner to client SLT

**Suggested visual:** A simple three-level diagram: Partner (client relationship) → ZenCloud (architecture delivery) → StudioSix (production). Clean hierarchy, no overlap.

## Slide 9 — IP and Confidentiality

**Slide title:** Clear IP and data boundaries

**Purpose:** Remove the ambiguity that kills commercial conversations before they start.

**Key points:**
- Engagement outputs (Board Packs, ADRs, Registers) are client IP upon delivery
- Velocity Framework methods, templates, and tool configuration are ZenCloud IP
- Partner-contributed methods and context remain partner IP
- No client data in public AI tools
- Generalised, anonymised patterns from engagements may become Velocity Framework IP — no client-identifiable content
- White-label engagements: ZenCloud not named, Velocity branded under partner's framework

**Suggested visual:** Three-column IP ownership table: Client IP / ZenCloud IP / Partner IP. Clean, simple.

## Slide 10 — Why Now

**Slide title:** Three forces making this the right moment

**Purpose:** Create urgency without pressure. Give the partner a reason to move.

**Key points:**
1. **AI adoption pressure:** Clients are being asked to adopt AI without a governance model for AI architecture decisions. Velocity provides that model.
2. **Governance theatre fatigue:** Boards and SLT are questioning the value of architecture functions that produce documents but cannot demonstrate decision traceability.
3. **Speed expectation:** Enterprise programs are expected to move faster. An artefact-first governance model cannot keep pace. A decision-first model can.

**Suggested visual:** Three concentric circles or three arrows converging — each labelled with the force. The Velocity logo at the intersection.

## Slide 11 — The Pilot Proposal

**Slide title:** Start with a bounded decision sprint

**Purpose:** Propose a low-risk entry point the partner can take to one client immediately.

**Key points:**
- Duration: 4 weeks

- Board presentation at close of sprint
- All outputs practitioner-reviewed

- Pilot demonstrates the Velocity model in the client's real context — not a hypothetical
