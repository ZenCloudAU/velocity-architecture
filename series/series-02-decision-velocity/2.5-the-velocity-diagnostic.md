# 2.5 — A Diagnostic for When Delivery Slows — Even When Things Look Clear

I've spent a lot of time inside organisations where decisions are documented, owners are named, authority formally exists — and delivery still struggles.

Not because people aren't trying. Not because teams lack capability. But because something structural is broken.

So instead of another opinion, here's something practical. A one-page diagnostic — part of the Velocity Architecture work — you can use when delivery feels slow, fragile, or dependent on heroics.

---

## The Velocity Diagnostic

When delivery stalls, the cause is almost never effort. It is almost always one of five systemic failures.

**1. Decisions Are Invisible**

Symptoms: rework, drift, late governance surprises.

Question: Can you see every architectural decision in one place?

Fix: one decision, one ADL, visible, owned, current.

**2. Decisions Are Clear — but Delivery Has No Pulse**

Symptoms: missing inputs, late requirements, inference-based delivery.

Question: Are required inputs owned and arriving on time?

Fix: assign accountable input owners, re-baseline when inputs don't arrive.

**3. Ownership Exists — but Authority Doesn't**

Symptoms: escalation loops, deferred trade-offs, delivery absorbing risk.

Question: Does the owner have binding decision rights?

Fix: delegate real authority, provide air cover, stop pretending ownership exists.

**4. Authority Exists — but Responsibility Is Deflected**

Symptoms: workshops instead of decisions, collaboration used as cover.

Question: Is authority being exercised — or avoided?

Fix: make escalations binding, measure decision follow-through.

**5. Authority Exists — but Leaders Push Forward Anyway**

Symptoms: rising risk, fading engagement, heroics replacing process.

Question: Is the system telling you to stop — but leadership accelerates?

Fix: treat stopping as a capability, pause, fix, or kill decisively.

---

## The 60-Second Test

Answer Yes / No:

- Are decisions visible and current?
- Are inputs owned and delivered on time?
- Do owners have real authority?
- Are escalations resolved with decisions?
- Is leadership willing to stop when the pulse is weak?

If any answer is No, velocity is structurally constrained.

---

Velocity is not about moving faster. It's about honouring reality sooner.

If the system can't support execution, pushing harder doesn't create progress. It creates deferred failure.
