## Executive Summary

Senior technology and transformation leaders operate under compounding pressure: AI adoption decisions without a governance model, programs diverging from design without a traceable explanation, architecture functions producing documents that do not reach the board, and delivery teams executing without architecture context.

These are not capability failures. They are decision-flow failures.

Velocity Executive Architecture FastStart is a structured advisory engagement that helps senior leaders rapidly diagnose where decision-flow breaks down, prioritise the interventions that will have the highest impact, and leave with a 90-day action plan that is specific, sequenced, and executable.

The FastStart supports existing teams and leadership. It does not replace formal decision authority or the accountability of the architecture and delivery functions already in place. It gives those functions a clearer operating model and a more visible decision trail.

## Ideal Trigger Situations

The FastStart is the right engagement when one or more of these conditions are present:

- A major program is approaching a governance gate and the decision trail is incomplete or untraceable
- AI adoption is underway or being evaluated with no architecture governance model in place
- A new executive has joined and needs a rapid baseline of architecture decision maturity before setting direction
- An architecture function is under scrutiny — from the board, a transformation office, or a cost review — and cannot easily demonstrate its governance value
- A merger, acquisition, or operating model change has created architecture and delivery misalignment
- A delivery program has stalled or diverged and the root cause cannot be traced to a recorded decision
- A partner engagement is beginning and the client's decision-flow maturity is unknown
- An architecture reset is needed before the next major investment cycle

## Delivery Approach

The FastStart operates under the Velocity Architecture Framework — applying the four-box operating model (EA, BA, SA, PMO) and the five cross-cutting lenses (Business Value, Data Readiness, Security Risk, AI Readiness, Governance & Traceability) to assess, diagnose, and prioritise.

**Assessment method:** Structured facilitation, document review, and targeted stakeholder interviews — not surveys or self-assessment forms.

**Output method:** Practitioner-synthesised analysis and recommendations. VAF Agentic AI assists with artefact drafting. All outputs reviewed and validated by Phil before delivery.

**Tone:** Direct, executive-grade, and specific. Findings name the gaps. Recommendations are sequenced and owned — not a list of possibilities.

## Activities

### Discovery and Assessment

- Executive sponsor session — context, priorities, and desired outcomes
- Decision Maturity Assessment across 12 dimensions (see `VELOCITY_DECISION_MATURITY_ASSESSMENT.md`)
- Review of submitted artefacts and governance documentation
- Targeted stakeholder interviews — architecture leads, delivery leads, PMO, data and security representatives as applicable
- Decision flow mapping — tracing how a recent major decision was actually made vs how it should have been

### Analysis and Synthesis

- Maturity scoring across the 12 VDMA dimensions with supporting evidence
- Identification of the top three to five decision-flow failure points with root cause analysis
- Assessment of which of the four boxes (EA, BA, SA, PMO) are most misaligned
- Cross-cutting lens assessment — where Business Value, Data Readiness, Security Risk, AI Readiness, and Governance are most exposed
- Prioritisation of interventions by impact and feasibility

### Action Plan Development

- 90-day action plan structured across three 30-day phases
- Each action: owner, success indicator, dependency, and connection to a Velocity engagement model where applicable
- Identification of the one architecture decision question that, if resolved, would have the highest downstream impact

### Output Production and Review

- Artefacts drafted with VAF Agentic AI assistance — practitioner reviewed before sharing
- Executive briefing prepared for delivery to the sponsoring executive and their leadership team
- Recommended follow-on engagement identified and framed

## 90-Day Action Plan Structure

The action plan is organised across three phases. Actions are specific, sequenced, and assigned to an owner.

### Phase 1 — Days 1 to 30: Stabilise

Purpose: Address the most urgent decision-flow failures and establish the foundation for the decision-first model.

Typical actions in this phase:
- Define and document the five most significant unresolved architecture decisions currently governing active programs
- Establish a decision register for the highest-priority program
- Agree an Architecture Decision Record format with the EA and SA leads
- Confirm data classification and AI governance boundaries for current programs
- Schedule the first architecture board review using the Velocity board pack format

### Phase 2 — Days 31 to 60: Structure

Purpose: Implement the decision-first operating model across the four-box framework in the priority domain.

Typical actions in this phase:
- Run the first Architecture Decision Sprint on the highest-priority unresolved decision question
- Align the PMO governance cadence with the architecture decision register
- Apply the five cross-cutting lenses to current major programs — identify where data, security, and AI readiness are most exposed
- Brief the board or SLT on the decision-first model and the 90-day plan progress

### Phase 3 — Days 61 to 90: Accelerate

Purpose: Extend the decision-first model, embed it in governance, and identify the next wave of interventions.

Typical actions in this phase:
- Complete the first full decision sprint and deliver the board pack
- Identify the practitioner capability gap — who in the architecture function needs Velocity certification or uplift
- Define the scope of the follow-on engagement (Advisory Overlay, Governance Reset, or Capability Uplift)
- Assess readiness for the Velocity AI Enablement or Data/Security readiness tracks

Each action in the plan is reviewed at the end of each 30-day phase. The plan is a live document — not a static deliverable.

## Follow-On Options

The FastStart is designed as a diagnostic and directional engagement. It identifies the highest-value follow-on and recommends one. The following options are available:

### Architecture Decision Sprint

A 4-week bounded engagement on the single highest-priority architecture decision question identified during the FastStart. Ends with a Board Pack, ADR, Trade-off Matrix, and Decision Register entry.

Applicable when: One unresolved decision is the primary blocker for a program or platform.

### VAF-SA Engagement Diagnostic

A structured review of how solution architecture is being practiced within a program or delivery function. Applies the VAF-SA method to assess how solution design decisions are being made, documented, and governed.

Applicable when: The FastStart reveals that solution architecture is the primary breakdown point — artefacts exist but decisions are implicit.

### PMO Governance Alignment

A structured engagement to align PMO reporting, governance cadence, and architecture decision registers. Produces a shared view of program health that includes architecture status, decision register currency, and upcoming decision gates.

Applicable when: The FastStart reveals that PMO and architecture are operating in parallel without shared artefacts or a common decision vocabulary.

### Velocity AI Enablement

A structured advisory engagement to assess AI readiness across the client's current architecture, data, and governance model — and to establish an AI architecture decision framework.

Applicable when: The FastStart reveals that AI adoption decisions are being made without architecture governance, or that the data and security foundations for AI are not yet in place.

### Data / Security / Business Architecture Readiness Assessment

Deep-dive diagnostic in one of the three cross-cutting framework lenses identified as highest-risk during the FastStart:

- **Data Readiness Assessment** — Data quality, lineage, source of truth, AI-readiness of current data
- **Security Architecture Assessment** — Security decision trail, control selection governance, exception management, AI security readiness
- **Business Architecture Assessment** — Capability impact of current architecture decisions, value stream alignment, benefits realisation confidence

Applicable when: The FastStart identifies one lens as the primary risk to program and investment outcomes.

## Success Criteria

The Velocity Executive Architecture FastStart is successful when:

1. The executive sponsor has a clear, evidence-based picture of where decision-flow breaks down in their organisation
2. The three to five highest-priority interventions are identified, sequenced, and owned
3. The 90-day action plan is specific enough to execute — not a list of principles
4. The executive can present the findings and plan to their leadership team without requiring additional analysis
5. The recommended follow-on engagement is agreed and scoped before the FastStart closes

**The FastStart has not succeeded if:**
- The output is a maturity score without a prioritised action plan
- The recommendations are generic rather than specific to the client's context
- The sponsoring executive cannot immediately identify what happens next and who is responsible for it
